Meet the Editors!

LuShuang Xu

LuShuang Xu, the Conference Director, is a manager in the Publishing Department on the Business Board of The Crimson. After a semester of associate directorship, she helped launch the inaugural spring journalism conference in April 2013 . As a department manager, she has assisted numerous local high schools and colleges in publishing their newspaper through The Crimson’s own printing presses. Originally from China and then California, LuShuang was a part of her high school newspaper and is currently studying Government and East Asian Studies. She is excited to welcome all of the new and returning journalists to the fall 2013 conference!

Kerry Flynn

Kerry Flynn, the Content Director of this year’s conference, is a news executive and daily design executive for The Crimson. With a passion for news and design, she helps oversee and coordinate multiple facets of the paper to create the daily product. Kerry was editor-in-chief and business manager of her high school newspaper for two years, and, at the same time, worked for her two-town newspaper as a correspondent during the academic year and summer. Kerry most recently interned for MONEY Magazine in NYC and freelanced for The Hartford Courant and The Day in New London, where she wrote a story that was picked up by the Associated Press. Hailing from Western Massachusetts, Kerry studies Environmental Science and Public Policy with a minor in Economics and plans to pursue metro reporting after graduation this May.

Rebecca Robbins

Rebecca Robbins is The Crimson’s managing editor, a position from which she oversees the production of The Crimson’s daily paper, three supplemental weekly sections, and a staff of over 200 writers, photographers, editors, and designers. She previously worked as an intern at the Hartford Courant and covered the Harvard College administration for The Crimson. She is looking forward to teaching what she’s learned about journalism through these experiences to middle and high school students at the Journalism Academy. Rebecca studies history as a rising senior at Harvard, and comes from Santa Barbara, Calif.

Bobby Samuels

Bobby Samuels is the current president of The Harvard Crimson. After joining The Crimson’s Sports Board in the Fall of 2010, Bobby covered the Harvard football and men’s hockey teams for two seasons each. In 2012, he served as Sports Chair and helped coordinate The Crimson’s sports coverage, which produced two different stories that earned national sports story of the year awards. A native of Pasadena, Calif., Bobby was the editor-in-chief of his high school newspaper. At Harvard, he is an economics concentrator and is also pursuing a secondary in Statistics.
