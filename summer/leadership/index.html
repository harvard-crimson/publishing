Meet the Editors!

Madeline Conway

Madeline Conway, the director of the Summer Journalism Academy, is a news writer for The Crimson. She has covered issues from the Boston Marathon bombings to academic integrity and the 2012 cheating scandal in her role as the undergraduate education beat reporter. Madeline is an incoming sophomore at Harvard studying history. She is originally from suburban Chicago, where she worked on her high school newspaper and developed her love for writing and newspaper design. She loves working with kids and teaching younger students about journalism, and she's looking forward to a great summer welcoming middle and high school students to The Crimson for the Summer Journalism Academy.

Maya Jonas-Silver

Maya Jonas-Silver, the Summer Journalism Academy coordinator, is an editor at The Crimson, directing and editing content for The Crimson’s newspaper, blog, and weekend magazine. She has worked as a camp counselor for the past five summers and is very excited to be working with the students of the Journalism Academy this summer. Maya is a rising senior at Harvard and studies human evolutionary biology. She lives in Dorchester and attended Boston Latin School, where she was the news editor of the school paper.

Rebecca Robbins

Rebecca Robbins is The Crimson’s managing editor, a position from which she oversees the production of The Crimson’s daily paper, three supplemental weekly sections, and a staff of over 200 writers, photographers, editors, and designers. She previously worked as an intern at the Hartford Courant and covered the Harvard College administration for The Crimson. She is looking forward to teaching what she’s learned about journalism through these experiences to middle and high school students at the Journalism Academy. Rebecca studies history as a rising senior at Harvard, and comes from Santa Barbara, Calif.

Bobby Samuels

Bobby Samuels is the current president of The Harvard Crimson. After joining The Crimson’s Sports Board in the Fall of 2010, Bobby covered the Harvard football and men’s hockey teams for two seasons each. In 2012, he served as Sports Chair and helped coordinate The Crimson’s sports coverage, which produced two different stories that earned national sports story of the year awards. A native of Pasadena, Calif., Bobby was the editor-in-chief of his high school newspaper. At Harvard, he is an economics concentrator and is also pursuing a secondary in Statistics.
